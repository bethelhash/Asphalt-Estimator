Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate asphalt tonnage?

To calculate asphalt tonnage, multiply the project area (length × width) by the thickness (in feet) and the asphalt density (typically 140-150 lbs/cubic foot). Divide by 2000 to convert to tons. Our calculator automates this process and accounts for waste factors and compaction variations.

What is the difference between loose and compacted asphalt?

Loose asphalt is freshly laid material that hasn't been compacted. Compacted asphalt is after rolling and compression, which increases density and reduces volume by approximately 20-30%. Our calculator accounts for this difference and helps you order the correct amount of material.

How many trucks do I need for my project?

The number of trucks needed depends on the total tonnage required and the truck's capacity. Our calculator divides your total tonnage by the selected truck's capacity and rounds up to the nearest whole number. Select your truck type and region for accurate capacity information.

What asphalt types are supported?

We support Hot Mix Asphalt (HMA), Warm Mix Asphalt (WMA), Porous Asphalt, Recycled Asphalt Pavement (RAP), Superpave Mix, Stone Matrix Asphalt (SMA), and Premium Dense Mix. Each type has different density characteristics that affect tonnage calculations.
